--- layout: default title: Dunn ---

Dunn et al. (2008) used phylogenomic methods to construct a comprehensive tree of life, focusing on figuring out relationships between metazoans. The study used large-scale molecular datasets, particularly expressed sequence tags (ESTs) and whole-genome sequences, to improve phylogenetic relationships between species and clades. By utilizing maximum likelihood and Bayesian inference methods, the researchers reconstructed evolutionary relationships with high statistical confidence. Dun et al.’s key findings showed strong support for the placement of ctenophores as a basal lineage, challenging earlier more traditional views of animal evolution. This work provided a framework for understanding metazoan diversification and the evolutionary origins of key biological traits.
