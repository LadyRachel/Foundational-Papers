--- layout: default title: Quiring ---

Quiring et al. (1994) investigated the genetic involvement of eye development by analyzing the eyeless gene in Drosophila melanogaster and homologs of the gene found in other species. Using molecular cloning, sequence comparisons, and functional analysis performed by transgenic experiments, Quiring demonstrated that the gene eyeless is homologous to the vertebrate Pax-6 gene. The key finding from this study was that eyeless/Pax-6 acts as a master control gene for eye formation and this was proved as expression in Drosophila induced the formation of eyes in non-ocular (without eyes normally) tissues. This discovery provided strong evidence for the deep evolutionary conservation of eye development mechanisms across animal phyla.
